Sri Lakshmi NrisimhaSabha ( SLNS ) has established its presence as a registered  Public Trust for the past three decades in Navi Mumbai. SLNS is a Spiritual, Cultural and Social Development organization. The Sabha is committed to extend its charitable services to the  public in religious activities, supporting music, dance and fine arts, providing medical services, spiritual development, preservation and propagation of Vedic studies, education, providing facilities for conducting social functions, marriage, seminars, medical camps and  other  socio- Cultural programs.

 

SABHA’S  MILESTONES

 

Acquirement of Land for developing Infra-structures

 

Determined to create infra-structure for  carrying out its Religious, Socio-Cultural and public welfare activities, SLNS  acquired a plot of land admeasuring about 3000 sqmtrs. of area atop Brahmagiri Hill in Nerul (West) on lease, allotted by M/s CIDCO Ltd. in 1991.

 

Construction of Sri BalajiMandir Complex

- a Landmark in Navi Mumbai

 

·      The Bhoomi Puja for Sri BalajiMandir Complex was officiated in the holy presence of His Holiness Jagadguru Sri SriSriKanchiKamakotiJayendraSaraswatiSankaracharya Swami in December 1991.

·      SLNS constructed  beautiful shrines , installing the adorable idols of  Lord Sri Balaji and Sri Padmavati Devi  therein and an impressive manimantapam.

·      Ceremonial consecration of the SHRINES was performed at the Holy Hands of  His Holiness  Sri SriSrirangaRamanuja Jeer Swamiji of TirumalaTirupati.

·      Subsequently in consistent manner,  shrines were built around Sri Balaji  Temple and Sri Padmavathi Devi , for Sri VidyaGanapati, Sri Vishwaksenar, Sri Yoga Anjaneya (Hanuman), Sri Lakshmi Nrisimha, Sri Ramanujacharya and Sri Sudarshanar.

·      SLNS built a Rajagopuram with Mahadwaram - an ornamental entrance of Sri BalajiMandir Complex. The Rajagopuram consists of around 200 ornate idols of Deities from various Divyadesams and AbhimanaSthalas. 

·      As a result of Sabha’s unrelenting efforts, this beautiful temple complex comprising of shrines artistically sculpted in granite stone based on South Indian Temple Architecture and iconography came into existence and remains a feast to the eyes.

·       The Worship routines are as per Sri Pancharatra Agama and gracefully guided by Sabha’s Patron Saint His Holiness Periya Jeer Swamiji of TirumalaTirupati.

·       Ourreligious procedures are equated with those followed in divyadesams in their grandeur and style by the visiting religious heads and vidvans

 

Commitment and Contribution towards ecological balancing

The Sabha maintains the garden plots in front of the temple and its rear side - the plots being entrusted on  lease by M/s CIDCO Ltd. for development purposes as a “Go Green Policy”. As such the Sabha contributes to the upkeep of the greenery around the premises and conserves the ecological splendor in the locality.

Promoting Charitable & Primary Medical Services

·       SLNS conducts Annadanam every day in the form of distributing Prasadam to public

·       The Sabha had set up a medical centre with a qualified doctor and extended medical support to many of the poor and construction workers, as far back as when the development of Nerul - West node was at primitive stage.

·       The Sabha provided Cardio-care ambulance on No-profit basis with the professional support from  Lakshadeep Hospital, Vashi.

·       Regular diagnostic and   medical camps were organized by the Sabha and supports like hearing-aid, spectacles etc. were distributed free of cost to the public.

·       SLNS is confident of enhancing all these activities soon after the construction of proposed Socio - Cultural Complex is complete.

Public Recognition of Sabha’s Services

The Public in large have contributed significantly in the development of SLNS by regular participation and generous contribution in its day to day activities and Annual Utsavams.

Sri VariSannidi

The Presiding Deity,  Lord Sri Venkateswara - Sri Balaji as fondly extolled by devotees - adorns the main sanctum sanctorum of the temple complex. The idol of Lord Sri Balaji  (moolavar)is sculpted at Mahabhalipuram, near Chennai, out of a single piece of granite stone, complying with the shilpashastras.  Devotees are delighted with the blissful darshan of Lord Sri Balaji - a repository of grace and compassion and an embodiment of vatsalya.

The kautukabera - a miniature of Sri Balajimoolavar , -sculpted in silver and enchanting utsavamurthis of Lord Sri Balaji along with His Devine Consorts Sri Devi and Bhoo Devi, yagnamurthis, balibera, Sri Ram - Sita -Lakshman, Sri Krishna - Rukmini and Sri Sudarshana - alltraditionally  sculpted in panchalohaare gracing the devotees with Their benevolentdarshan in the main shrine.

A spacious mani-mantapam overlooks and enjoins the sanctorum. DwaraPalakas and Sri Garuda are installed in the mani-mantapam.

Sri PadmavatiThaayaarSannidi

Sri PadmavatiThaayarSannidi was the first ever shrine constructed in the Temple Complex as far back as Lord Sri Balaji was worshipped in balalayam.

The utsava idol of Thaayar chiseledin panchalohawas installed and worshipped in the shrine fully constructed with stone even prior to installation and consecration of the moolavar idol in the year 1999 - thereby indicating Thaayaar’s precedence in gracing the devotees thereby exhibiting Her ever flowing compassion towards the astikas.

Sri Bhoga Lakshmi Thaayaar gracing in a silver idol replicating MoolavarThaayaar and  Sri Goda Devi in a panchalohavigrham, revered as AandaalNachiyar, also bless the devotees in this Sannidi.

Devotees throng in large numbers on Fridays and other auspicious days for darshan.

Sri Yoga AnjaneyarSannidi

Sri Hanuman graces the devotees in His Yoga form adorned with Shanku and Chakra in His hands - the form similar to that at Sholingar. The impressive idol chiseled in stone is enchanting with face emanating both bhakti towards Sri Rama and benediction on the bhaktas.

 

An impressive Hanuman in utsavavigraham and magnificent Sri Ram Parivar sculpted inpanchaloha shower blessings and prosperities on the devotees.Astikas regularly offer their prayers to Sri Hanuman with Vadamala and garlands made with Betel leaves on every Saturday evening.

 

The structure of this Sannidi in front of Sri Lakshmi Nrisimhar Shrine reminds the devotee of  Sri Hanuman offering His prayers to Sri Nrisimha - both enshrined atop two hills facing each other in the temple town of Sholingar.

Sri VishwaksenarSannidi

Sri Vishwaksenaras  the chieftain SrimanNarayana’sdivyaparivara graces the devotees and He is auspiciously installed in a location to the left of Lord Sri BalajiSannidi ...

 

The location of the sannidi was pre-determined and hinted by Sri Vishwaksena Himself during the inaugural patishta of Lord Sri Balaji in June 1999, by gracing the occasion with His appearance in the form of a snake coming out of Sri VariSannadi soon after the pratishta and peacefully disappearing at this site. His Holiness Sri SrirangaRamanuja Jeer Swami who was gracing the pratishta rituals took cue from the unusual incident and revealed the sankalpa of Sri Vishwaksena choosing His abode in the Temple Complex at this location.

 

Adorned with the commanding staff gracefully, Sri Vishwaksena radiates His preparedness in carrying out the orders and divyasankalpa of the Presiding Deity Lord Sri Balajiand conferprosperous fortune to all of His devotees.

NavagrahamSannidi

The significance of Navagraha worship has been stressed by ancient saints and maharishis and references are available in the sacred writings, one of them being the 'MahaPrayaschithaGrantha'. It states that by the worship of Navagrahas, the planets which are in auspicious situations offer increased fruits of benefits for one's actions while the planets which occupy less desirous situations tend to downsize the evil effects of a person's karma.

 

Keeping in view of the connotation of Navagraha worship and for the purpose of providing the much needed worship facilities to the devotees, the Sabha has installed navagrahas in a separate sannidi and regular pujas and periodical havans are performed since then.
